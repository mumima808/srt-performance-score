## The MIDI Analogy

MIDI changed music production.

It separated the *meaning* of sound from the sound itself. The timbre is decided by the synthesizer. MIDI only carries timing and pitch. That is why it has remained the HUB connecting every device for decades.

SRT can do the same thing.

| MIDI | SRT |
|------|-----|
| Pitch · timing | Words · timecode |
| Timbre is decided by the synth | Visuals are decided by DaVinci |
| Voice is decided by the singer | Voice is decided by TTS |
| Connects every device | Connects every tool |

And there is one decisive difference from MIDI.

**SRT is human-readable.**

MIDI is binary. No one reads it directly. But SRT is plain text. Anyone can write it. Edit it. Version-control it on GitHub.

## How the Discovery Happened

Video editing tools have been trapped inside the timeline paradigm for a long time.

Tracks on the vertical axis. Time on the horizontal. This design carried the physical constraints of the recording studio directly into the digital world. DaVinci Resolve, DAW, DTM — all of them stand on essentially the same philosophy.

The problem is here.

**Too many people run out of energy learning the tools, and never reach the point of creating.**

Vrew has a unit called "a chunk of words." It packages still images, short clips, and audio together — with the word as the unit. You can place "silence" before and after each chunk.

That was the moment.

This is not a time axis. This is **a time-control device with the word as its unit.** SRT can be its blueprint.

---

## Designing Silence

This is a technique professionals have always known — and rarely put into words.

An idol on stage: *"Are you ready? — Yeah!"*
The pause before a comedian's punchline.
The rakugo performer's *"Eeeh…"* that pulls the audience forward.
The hip-hop MC who gets 10,000 people to finish the lyric.

All of these are the same mechanism. **Utterance → Silence → Response.** The designed gap in time turns the audience into a performer.

In SRT timecode terms — the blank gap is not empty. It is a cue.

DJs do the same thing. Filters and layers build the tension, pulling the crowd to the edge. Then the drop. The relief is physical. This too is timecode structure. It can be written as an SRT file.

**DJs have always been composing performance scores. They just called it "reading the room."**

---

## Why the DJ Controller and Pad Sampler Are Closest

DAW and DTM cannot escape the tyranny of the timeline.

But the DJ controller and pad sampler are different. The pad triggers a *chunk.* Instead of placing sound on a time axis, it triggers a meaningful unit. The order and the gaps are decided in the moment.

You can perform without knowing music theory.

Vrew's "chunk of words" is the same philosophy. Without knowing editing theory, you can design time simply by arranging language.
